1. Introduction

This Business Continuity Plan (BCP) serves as an extension of the Critical Incident Policy and Procedures for Athena Schools. While the Critical Incident Policy focuses on immediate response to emergencies, this BCP addresses the sustained operations and recovery of educational services during and after prolonged disruptions.

The UAE's geographical location and current geopolitical context present unique challenges, including potential missile and drone attacks, disruptions to maritime routes through the Straits of Hormuz, and regional conflicts that may affect school operations. This plan provides a framework for maintaining essential educational functions during such emergencies.

2. Purpose and Scope

Purpose

This Business Continuity Plan aims to:

  • Ensure the continuation of essential educational services during emergencies
  • Minimise disruption to teaching and learning
  • Protect student and staff welfare during prolonged crises
  • Maintain stakeholder confidence through effective management of disruptions
  • Comply with UAE regulatory requirements and international best practices
  • Facilitate swift recovery and return to normal operations

Scope

This plan applies to:

  • All school premises and school-sponsored activities
  • All students, staff, contractors, and visitors
  • Extended emergencies as defined in the Critical Incident Policy
  • Specific scenarios including pandemic, severe weather, bereavement, evacuation due to fire/earthquake/flooding/storm damage, and geopolitical conflicts

3. Integration with Critical Incident Policy

This Business Continuity Plan builds upon the foundation established in the Critical Incident Policy and Procedures. The relationship between the two documents is as follows:

  • Critical Incident Policy: Focuses on immediate response, safety protocols, and short-term management of emergencies.
  • Business Continuity Plan: Addresses sustained operations, alternative delivery methods, and recovery strategies.

The transition from crisis response to business continuity will be determined by the Critical Incident Management Team (CIMT) based on the nature and duration of the incident.

6. Continuity Strategies

Coordination between on-site and remote teaching – AWPS will ensure that curriculum delivery remains consistent for both in-school and remote learners through coordinated planning and communication among teaching staff.

Alternative Locations

  • Identification of backup facilities – AWPS will identify suitable alternative locations that may be used temporarily if the main school premises become unavailable due to emergency conditions.
  • Memoranda of Understanding (MOU) with ‘sister’ or partner schools – AWPS will establish formal agreements with partner institutions to allow shared use of facilities during prolonged disruptions.
  • Use of community facilities – AWPS may utilise appropriate community venues such as halls or educational centres where safe and practical learning spaces are required.
  • Rapid deployment requirements for temporary learning spaces – AWPS will maintain plans and logistical arrangements for quickly establishing temporary classrooms or learning areas if needed.
  • Security and safety assessment protocols for alternative sites – AWPS will conduct risk and safety assessments to ensure any temporary learning location meets safeguarding, security, and operational standards.

Staff Deployment Strategies

  • Cross-training for critical roles – AWPS will ensure that key staff members are trained to perform essential functions outside their usual responsibilities to maintain operational continuity.
  • Remote working protocols – AWPS will provide guidance and technological support to enable staff to perform their duties effectively while working remotely during disruptions.
  • Staff redeployment procedures – AWPS may temporarily reassign staff to priority roles or areas where additional support is required during emergency conditions.
  • Temporary staffing arrangements – AWPS will maintain procedures for engaging temporary or substitute staff when regular personnel are unavailable.
  • Support for staff in evacuation scenarios – AWPS will provide appropriate guidance and assistance for staff who may be required to relocate or evacuate due to emergency or diplomatic requirements.

Supply Chain Resilience

  • Critical supplier identification – AWPS will identify essential suppliers whose services are necessary to maintain school operations during disruptions.
  • Alternative supplier arrangements – AWPS will establish secondary supplier options to reduce reliance on a single provider and minimise supply interruptions.
  • Stockpiling of essential materials – AWPS will maintain reasonable reserves of critical materials and resources required for safe school operation.
  • Local sourcing strategies during disruptions – AWPS will prioritise locally available resources where possible to ensure continued access to essential goods during supply chain interruptions.
  • Digital resource alternatives – AWPS will increase the use of digital materials and resources to reduce dependence on physical supplies when disruptions affect logistics or distribution.

* Strategies and procedures comply with expectations by regulators and inspectors in the UAE, including authorities such as the UAE Ministry of Education and Sharjah Private Education Authority.

Additional Operational Elements

1 Business Continuity Activation Levels

To ensure a consistent and structured response during disruptions, AWPS will implement the following escalation framework.

Level Situation Example Scenarios Response
Level 1 – Monitoring Minor disruption with minimal operational impact Localised technical issue, temporary weather disruption Internal monitoring and routine management
Level 2 – Limited Disruption School operations partially affected Partial campus closure, transport disruption Limited continuity measures activated
Level 3 – Major Disruption Full campus closure required Pandemic outbreak, major flooding, infrastructure failure Full Business Continuity Plan activated
Level 4 – Regional/National Emergency Widespread disruption affecting multiple institutions Regional conflict, national emergency directive Coordination with authorities and remote operations

The School Principal and the Critical Incident Management Team (CIMT) will determine the activation level based on the severity and expected duration of the disruption.

4 Emergency Quick Guide for Teachers

This one-page guide can be issued to all teaching staff.

Quick Guide for Teachers

If School Closure Occurs

Teachers should:

  • Check official staff communication channels immediately.
  • Prepare remote learning lessons according to the school’s digital learning protocol.
  • Upload lesson materials to the designated learning platform.
  • Record student attendance and participation.
  • Report safeguarding concerns immediately.

During Remote Learning

Teachers must:

  • Maintain regular contact with students.
  • Provide clear instructions for assignments.
  • Ensure lessons are inclusive and accessible.
  • Monitor student wellbeing and engagement.

Safeguarding Reminder

Teachers must continue to follow the school’s safeguarding procedures and report concerns to the designated safeguarding lead without delay.
